Internal Revenue Code(IRC)

The Internal Revenue Code (IRC) is the federal tax code of the United States. It is a comprehensive set of laws and regulations that govern the administration and enforcement of the U.S. federal tax system. The IRC covers a wide range of topics related to taxation, including rules for calculating income, deductions, credits, tax rates, and the procedures for filing tax returns and paying taxes.

The IRC is a codification of the tax laws enacted by Congress and is divided into subtitles, chapters, and sections. It is regularly updated to reflect changes in tax policy, economic conditions, and other factors that may affect the administration of the tax code.
