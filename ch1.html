Three misconceptions about Computer Science:
1.Computer science is the study of computers.
2.Computer science is the study of how to write computer programs
3.Computer science is the study of the uses and applications of computers and software.
Briefly Define:
1.Computer Science: the study of computation, automation, and information
2.algorithm: a repetitive procedure for solving a math problem.
3.sequential operation: When the first task is finnished. It moves on to the next one.
4.conditional operation: task is assigned when a question or questions are answered
5.iterative operation: "looping" repetitive tasks
6.stored program computer: a computer that use codes to store programs instead of wire
7.programmer: one who writes programms using high-level language
8.integrated circuit: reduced-size computers that has transistors, resistors, and capacitors were photographically etched onto a piece of silicon.
Briefly describe thesecomputer generations:
1.First generation computer: used by only trained personnel
2.Second generation computer: major change in size and simplify the complexity
3.Third generation computer: continued miniaturization, integrated circuit
4.Fourth generation computer:first microcomputer, computer came to everyday lives
5.Fifth generation computer: computers today. massive cloud storage, self-driving automation, etc.

FORTRAN:

  1. What does the acronym FORTRAN stand for? A:Formula Translation
  2. When was FORTRAN developed? A:1957
  3. What was it used for? A: The first computer language standard.
  4. Is FORTRAN still being used today? If so, what for? A: Yes. It is still present in scientific computing such as weather and ocean studies

COBOL:

  1. What does the acronym COBOL stand for? A:Common Business Oriented Language
  2. When was COBOL developed? A:1959
  3. What was it used for? A: It was originally used for business such as finance and human resources
  4. Is COBOL still being used today? If so, what for? A: Yes. It is still used as business applications today.
